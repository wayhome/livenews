文章摘要

Frank Herbert’s *Dune* (1965) draws inspiration from the story of Lawrence of Arabia, particularly in the character of Paul Atreides, who, like Lawrence, is a foreign figure immersing himself in a desert culture to help overthrow oppressors. The 1962 film *Lawrence of Arabia* presents Lawrence as a "white savior" assisting oppressed peoples, a theme that resonates with Paul's role among the Fremen on Arrakis. Herbert, who researched extensively including reading Lawrence's *Seven Pillars of Wisdom*, was interested in desert cultures and religions, and saw the potential for outsiders to manipulate a culture. However, *Dune* differs from Lawrence's story in key ways, including its portrayal of women and religion, and the complexity of its characters. While *Lawrence of Arabia* implies a "white savior" narrative, *Dune* offers a more nuanced exploration of this theme, sparking ongoing debate about its portrayal of colonialism and heroism.

Hacking the Xbox 360 Hypervisor Part 2: The Bad Update Exploit

文章摘要
